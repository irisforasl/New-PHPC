DH Clinic Licence Application Commencement

The Department of Health commenced accepting clinic licence applications under Cap. 633 from 13 October 2025, marking the transition of all private clinics to the mandatory licensing regime. Clinics must demonstrate robust patient data security and privacy practices. CMEs are personally accountable for compliance during DH on-site inspections.

Source: ORPHF ↗
⚕️
CME's Compliance Role · 醫務主管的合規責任
Chief Medical Executive — Compliance Obligations Under Cap. 633

Under Cap. 633, the Chief Medical Executive (CME) is appointed to take charge of the clinic's day-to-day administration and to help ensure that the clinic operates in compliance with applicable regulatory requirements. In practical terms, this means the CME should oversee whether appropriate systems, policies, and controls are in place to protect patient information, maintain confidentiality, and support proper record handling.

During a Department of Health inspection, it is not enough to rely on verbal assurances that staff are careful. The clinic should be able to show documented evidence of its compliance arrangements — including written policies, access controls, audit trails, staff procedures, and other safeguards that demonstrate how sensitive patient information is protected.

Where a patient portal or similar digital system is used, it should support compliance by providing structured, documented, and auditable controls. Features such as encryption, access logs, and role-based permissions can help demonstrate that the clinic has put in place practical measures to protect patient data and meet regulatory expectations.

Source: ORPHF — New Licensing Scheme ↗

Data Boundary — Explicit by Design · 數據邊界設計

The PHPC Patient Portal is a patient access layer and healthcare interoperability layer — it sits above your existing clinic management system without replacing it. It does not collect, store, or analyse any commercial data about the clinic. Business intelligence is generated solely from the clinic's own management system, which the clinic owns and controls entirely. The portal lets patients see their data. It does not let us see your business.
